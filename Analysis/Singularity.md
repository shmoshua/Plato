#Definition #Analysis 

> [!definition]
> Let $A\subseteq \mathbb{R}^d$ be non-empty. 
> 1. A point $p\in A$ is ***non-singular*** if there exists open sets $U,V\subseteq \mathbb{R}^d$ and an analytic isomorphism $f:U\to V$ s.t. $$f(U\cap A)=\{ (x_{1:r},0) \}\cap V$$
> 	If $p$ is non-singular for all $p\in A$, $A$ is called ***non-singular***.
> 2. A point $p\in A$ is ***singular*** if it's not non-singular. The set of singular points in $A$ is denoted by $\text{Sing}(A)$.
